Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

  1. Keep Your Devices Updated

    2. Always use antivirus software and make sure your computer, browser, and apps are up to date. Set them to update automatically to stay protected against new threats.

  2. Use Strong and Unique Passwords

    3. Create long passwords (at least 10 characters) with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts. Use a password manager to safely store and manage them.

  3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

    4. Add an extra layer of security by using two-factor authentication. This could be a code sent to your phone or fingerprint login. Even if someone gets your password, 2FA can block them.

  4. Think Before You Click

    5. Don’t click on links or attachments in emails or texts unless you're sure they're safe. Scammers often pretend to be your bank or a trusted company to trick you into sharing personal information.

  5. Protect Your Personal Info

    6. Treat your personal data like cash. Don’t give out your Social Security number, account numbers, or passwords unless you’re sure who you’re dealing with. Be cautious with unexpected calls, texts, or emails.

  6. Monitor Your Accounts Regularly

    7. Check your bank and credit card statements often. Watch for any charges or changes you don’t recognize. Sign up for alerts from your bank for added protection.

  7. Back Up Your Files

    8. Keep copies of important documents and files on an external hard drive or secure cloud storage. If something happens to your computer, you won’t lose everything.

  8. Report Suspicious Activity Immediately

    9. If you notice anything strange—unauthorized transactions, password changes, or missing emails—contact your bank right away. Fast action can prevent further damage.
